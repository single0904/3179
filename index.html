COVID-19 IN MALAYSIA
COVID-19 short for "Coronavirus Disease 2019," is a highly contagious
respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It was first
identified in China in late 2019 and has since spread globally,
leading to a pandemic.

This data visualization provides a snapshot of the COVID-19 pandemic's
impact on Malaysia. It offers a visual representation of key metrics
such as the total number of cases, daily new cases, recoveries, and
fatalities.

The charts and graphs help to track the progression of the virus, highlighting trends, spikes, and critical milestones in the country's response to the pandemic.

In this visualization, 3 datasets were used to illustrate the COVID-19 situation in Malaysia from February 2020 to Decemeber 2022. The first dataset consists of daily number of cases in each state. The second and third dataset is the daily number of tests and deaths in Malaysia.

THE TOTAL NUMBER OF CASES AND THE TOTAL NUMBER OF RECOVERED CASES IN EACH STATE

The grouped bar chart below shows that Selangor, Sabah and Sarawak has the
highest number of cases in Malaysia.

Furthermore, it also shows that the fatality rate is very low in all states as the total
number of recovered cases is very similar to the total number of cases.

NUMBER OF NEW CASES PER 100,000 PEOPLE OF EACH STATE FROM FEBRUARY 2020 TO DECEMBER 2022

Even from the start of 2020, Selangor and Sabah has been the states with the highest
number of new cases in Malaysia.

Moreover, the number of new cases skyrocketed throughout 2021 until June 2022.
It then gradually falls out and plateaus.

NUMBER OF DEATHS OF PEOPLE WITH DIFFERENT LEVELS OF VACCINATION

Since the vaccination didn't arrive until 2021, the people who passed in 2020
were mostly unvaccinated.

Although a large number of people who passed in 2021 and 2022 were also
unvaccinated, people who were partially vaccinated(only 1 shot) contributed
quite a decent sum of deaths in 2021.

However, 2022 shows that fully vaccinated indivuduals has a higher fatality rate
from COVID-19 in 2022.

CUMULATIVE SUM OF NEW CASES OF PEOPLE WITH DIFFERENT LEVELS OF VACCINATION

Between June 2021 to September 2021, we can see that individuals who were partially
vaccinated had a higher infection rate than those who were fully vaccinated, however
it eventually plateaus throughout the end of 2021.

In 2022, the infection rate of fully vaccinated individuals was actually the highest
among the others, indicating that most of the new cases originated from people
who were fully vaccinated.

THE TYPES OF TESTS FOR COVID-19 USED IN MALAYSIA

There are two types of test used in Malaysia for COVID-19.
The Real-time Reverse Transcription Polymerase Chain Reaction (RT-PCR)
and the Antigen Rapid Test Kits (RTK-Ag).

About 75% of people in 2020 used the PCR test to effectivey test for COVID-19.
But in 2021 and 2022, majority of the population use the RTK tests
and only less than 20% of the population in 2022 uses the PCR test
to test for COVID-19.
